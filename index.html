Understanding "Revenue Neutral" - What It Really Means

What is Revenue Neutral?

A "revenue neutral" rate means the county collects the same total amount of tax dollars as last year, even though property values increased.

Simple Example:

If all home values doubled, a revenue neutral rate would be half the previous rate.

The Problem With Revenue Neutral

  • Doesn't account for inflation
  • Ignores rising costs of services
  • Can lead to service cuts
  • Doesn't consider population growth

What Services Would Be Affected?

Emergency Services

  • Longer 911 response times
  • Reduced fire department staffing
  • Fewer police patrols

Infrastructure

  • Delayed road repairs
  • Reduced snow removal
  • Postponed infrastructure updates

Community Services

  • Limited park maintenance
  • Reduced community programs
  • Fewer neighborhood services

Housing Inflation in Wyandotte County

Why Are Home Values Rising?

  • Increased demand for housing
  • Limited housing supply
  • Low interest rates (historical)
  • Regional economic growth

Impact on Homeowners

  • Higher property taxes
  • Increased insurance costs
  • Greater equity in homes
  • Higher cost of living

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did my taxes go up when the rate went down? ▼
While tax rates decreased slightly (1.85%), property values increased significantly (46% on average). Since taxes are based on your property's value, the large increase in value outweighed the small decrease in rates.
How is my property tax calculated? ▼
Your property tax is calculated by:
  1. Taking your home's assessed value
  2. Multiplying it by 11.5% (Kansas residential assessment rate)
  3. Multiplying that by the mill levy rate
  4. Dividing by 1000
