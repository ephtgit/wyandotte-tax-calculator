Understanding "Revenue Neutral" Rates

What Officials Say

"Revenue neutral means keeping the same amount of total revenue as last year."

  • Same total dollars collected
  • Lower tax rates
  • Maintains current budget

The Reality

With inflation at 7-9% and rising costs:

  • Same dollar amount buys less services
  • Equipment costs more to maintain
  • Staff wages fall behind inflation

Real Impact on County Services

Emergency Services:
  • Longer 911 response times
  • Delayed equipment updates
  • Reduced police patrols
Infrastructure:
  • Only emergency pothole repairs
  • Delayed park maintenance
  • Postponed facility updates
Community Programs:
  • Reduced community center hours
  • Cancelled youth programs
  • Limited senior services

Did You Know?

Even with "revenue neutral" rates, most homeowners will pay more in taxes due to increased home values. However, the county will have less purchasing power to provide services due to inflation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did my taxes go up when the rate went down? +
While tax rates decreased slightly, property values increased significantly (average 46%). Since taxes are based on your property's value, the large value increase typically results in higher taxes despite lower rates. For example, if your home value increased from $100,000 to $146,000, you'll pay more even with a slightly lower tax rate.
What is the 11.5% assessment rate? +
In Kansas, residential properties are taxed on 11.5% of their total value. This means if your home is worth $200,000, your taxes are calculated on $23,000 (11.5% of $200,000). This assessed value is then multiplied by the mill levy rate to determine your actual tax amount.
What does "revenue neutral" really mean? +
"Revenue neutral" means the county collects the same total dollar amount as the previous year. However, this doesn't account for inflation or rising costs. While it sounds good, it actually results in service cuts because the same dollar amount buys less each year due to inflation.
How can I challenge my property value? +
You can appeal your property valuation through the Wyandotte County Appraiser's Office. The appeal must be filed within 30 days of receiving your valuation notice. You'll need to provide evidence supporting your claim, such as recent sales of comparable properties or an independent appraisal.
